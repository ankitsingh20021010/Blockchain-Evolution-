Evolution of Blockchain

Key milestones from idea to global infrastructure

1991

First blockchain concept proposed for secure digital time-stamping.

1992

Merkle Trees added, enabling multiple records per block efficiently.

2000

Theory of cryptographically secured chains formally published.

2004

Reusable Proof of Work introduced to prevent double spending.

2008

Bitcoin whitepaper defined decentralized peer-to-peer blockchain.

2009

Bitcoin launched — first real blockchain in production.

2014

Blockchain separated from crypto, enabling enterprise adoption.

2015

Ethereum launched — smart contracts became real.

2016

DAO hack proved security is critical in smart contracts.

2017

Bitcoin gained legal and global mainstream recognition.

2018

Crypto hype cooled, real-world blockchain use gained focus.

2019

Blockchain entered enterprise cloud platforms like AWS.

2020

Stablecoins and DeFi reshaped digital finance.

2022

Ethereum shifted to Proof of Stake, cutting energy use massively.

2023

Blockchain matured into Web3, DAOs, and real-world systems.
